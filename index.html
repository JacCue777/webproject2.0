Piano 101

Learning how to play piano and reading music is hard. This website is here to guide beginners on where to start when wanting to learn how to play piano!

The necessities and resources

  • A piano
  • Piano stool
  • Beginner sheet music
  • Musecore
  • Pianocoda

Not sure what kind of piano too get?

Check out our table to compare options.

Types of Piano Typical Price Range Noteable differences
Digital Keyboard $500 - $1,000
  • Beginner friendly
  • Cheapest option
  • Portable
  • Usually made of plastic with smaller lighter keys
  • Versitile
  • Sound quality isnt the best but a great starting option
Digital Piano $500 - $6,000
  • Larger and heavier than keyboards
  • Mimicks an acoustic piano sound better than keyboard
  • You dont need to hire a professional to transport
  • heavier, larger keys made to imitate acoustic piano
  • Versatile but less than keyboard
  • Entire 88 keys
Acoustic Piano (upright) $3,000 - $25,000
  • A richer sound then both digital keyboard and piano
  • Transportation need to be done professionally
  • Maintence must be upkept
  • Ill advised to purchase a used piano as the often need expensive repairs
  • No electricity is required

Now to some basic information and handy charts that can help ease the learning process and be used in future playing!

The piano uses base and treble clef on sheet music. Base clef notes will usually be used with your left hand and treble clef notes will be used with your right
